About The Olympiad League

The Games Are Back!

In order to foster a greater cooperation between nations, as well as to allow for the creative outlet of national animosity in a more civilized manner, Queen Zoe Marmaromenos of the Hellenes has established a new commission into restoring the ancient Greek games that had occured in antiquity, known simply as the Olympiad. The official Olympiad Commission will be manned by representatives from each nation that wishes to participate in the games, to pit their best athletes in tests of strength, dexterity, endurance and skill to determine who truly are the best of the best. Each nation will have equal representation within the games, with no group or ideology receiving favor or preference over any other. Indeed, while the nations of the world may uses the Olympiad as a measure of their national pride against their rivals, the games are instead meant to challenge those individuals seeking to overcome their own physical prowess and performance to become the best in the world at what they do.

About the Games

The Olympiad has always been about sportsmanship, comeraderie, and competition. As such, to stay within this theme, the games will not be offering great rewards to the victors. To incentivize participation, 5k resources will go to the nation with the best medal count, 2.5k resources to the first runner up, and 1k resources to the third runner up.

The true reward for excellence will be the right to brag to the world that your nation, your citizens, your government is truly superior to the competition. That no matter what bluster or howling they may do, that when it comes down to the individual man, it was YOU who was proven superior.

About the Hosts

A vote will be cast of 5 nations who proposed as host cities.
Previous hosts from the past 2 Olympiad are excluded.

Second Place Host gets the Winter Olympiad and the other 50% of the entry fees
NATIONS AT WAR 1 YEAR PRIOR TO THE OLYMPIAD ARE DISQUALIFIED AS HOST.

F.A.Q

  • How do I join the Olympiad League?

    To participate in the Olympiad, all you have to do is send the Entry Fee to the Olympiad Commission (Greece), and you will be entered into elligible events. You may also pay a reduced fee to only be elligible for certain events of your choosing, but you are not guaranteed to place in these events.

  • What is the reward for winning the KRPU Olympiad?

    Bragging rights. Not everything in Roleplay needs to have a physical reward. You get to annoy your rivals who keep trash talking you and tell them who is the better man.

  • No, seriously, what's the reward for winning?

    Greece funds the events out of pocket. If you want to pay higher entry fees for a purse, coordinate with other nations to request such a feature.

  • Where are the Olympiad held?

    The host cities will be elected by straw polls every 24 days. First place winner is awarded the Summer Olympiad, and the runner up gets the Winter Olympiad. Ties will be broken by the Commission or by a new strawpoll.

  • Why can't I host the Olympiad?

    In order to host the Olympiad, you must:

    • Not be in a war 1 year in advance of the election, nor plan to be at war during the event.
    • Must have been one of the first 5 countries proposed or offered to host the Olympiad
    • Not have been a Summer or Winter Olympiad host in the past 32 days (past 2 events)

  • How will winners be determined?

    The Commission has developed a simple random generator which shuffles every participant in the participation list to a random spot. This list is then shuffled six additional times, before the final placing list is produced. The first nation in the list is awarded first place, and the two subsequent runners up given second and third in order of their place on the list.

  • Why shuffle the list seven times to determine a winner?

    It has been scientifically proven that the minimum number of full shuffles that must be done to a deck of cards to ensure it is adequately randomized is seven. As such, the list is also randomized seven times to allow for an appropriate amount of randomization and fairness to all participants. After seven shuffles, each additional shuffle has too strong of a diminishing return to be of note.
